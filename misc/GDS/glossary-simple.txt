anthropogenic microfeature - a)  A small, human-made “microfeature” on the earth’s surface or in shallow water, with a characteristic shape and range in composition of unconsolidated earthy, organic, human-transported materials, or rock and that is typically too small to delineate at common soil survey scales (e.g., Order 2: > 1: 10,000 and < 1:24,000).  Anthropogenic microfeatures can be either constructional accumulations (e.g. railroad bed) or destructional voids (e.g. ditch) in origin.  b) A category in NASIS and the Geomorphic Description System for human-derived or modified “landforms”.  Compare – anthropogenic landform, anthroscape.  SW
